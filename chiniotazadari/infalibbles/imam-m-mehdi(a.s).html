Hazrat Imam Mahdi (AS) is the twelfth and final Imam of Shia Islam. He is also known as Imam Al-Mahdi, Imam Al-Hujjah, or Sahib al-Zaman, which means "the master of the age." According to Shia Islamic belief, Imam Mahdi (AS) was born in Samarra, Iraq, in 868 AD, and is still alive, but is currently in occultation, or hidden from the public eye.

Shia Muslims believe that Imam Mahdi (AS) is the ultimate savior and will appear before the end of time to establish a just and peaceful society. His arrival is considered to be a major event that will mark the end of the world as we know it and the beginning of a new era of justice and peace.

Imam Mahdi (AS) is believed to have supernatural powers and knowledge and is considered to be a link between humanity and God. Shia Muslims hold a special place in their hearts for Imam Mahdi (AS) and frequently pray for his return.

Although Imam Mahdi (AS) is currently in occultation, Shia Muslims believe that he is present among them and that they can communicate with him through prayer and supplication. His message of peace, justice, and compassion continues to inspire Muslims around the world, and his legacy remains a vital part of Shia Islamic tradition.

  • Shia Muslims believe that Imam Mahdi (AS) was born in 868 AD in Samarra, Iraq, to the eleventh Imam, Hassan Askari (AS), and his wife, Lady Narjis Khatoon (SA).
  • According to Shia Islamic belief, Imam Mahdi (AS) went into occultation or hiding in 941 AD and is still in occultation, waiting for the time when he will reappear to establish justice and equity on earth.
  • Imam Mahdi (AS) is believed to be the final savior and will emerge before the Day of Judgment to restore justice and establish a society based on the principles of Islam.
  • Shia Muslims believe that Imam Mahdi (AS) is the rightful successor of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and is the twelfth and final Imam in the line of Shia Imams.
  • During his occultation, Imam Mahdi (AS) has appointed four representatives, called Naibs or deputies, who act as his representatives and communicate his messages to the Shia community.
  • Shia Muslims believe that Imam Mahdi (AS) will reappear with Prophet Isa (AS), who is also known as Jesus Christ in Christianity, to fight against evil and establish justice on earth.
  • Shia Muslims hold a special place in their hearts for Imam Mahdi (AS) and pray for his reappearance. They believe that his reappearance will bring an end to oppression, injustice, and tyranny in the world.
