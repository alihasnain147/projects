Hazrat Muhammad (PBUH) is the last prophet and messenger of Allah (God) in Islam. He was born in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, in 570 AD. He is considered the most influential person in history, with his teachings and practices shaping the lives and beliefs of over 1.8 billion Muslims worldwide.

Muhammad (PBUH) was known for his exceptional character, wisdom, and leadership. He lived a simple life and devoted himself entirely to spreading the message of Islam, which emphasized the worship of one God (Allah) and the importance of living a righteous and just life.

During his lifetime, Muhammad (PBUH) faced numerous challenges and opposition from the ruling tribes in Mecca, who were resistant to his teachings. Despite this, he remained steadfast in his beliefs and continued to spread the message of Islam.

Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation of the Quran, the holy book of Islam, at the age of 40, while he was in a cave on Mount Hira. This event marks the beginning of his prophethood and the emergence of Islam.

Throughout his life, Muhammad (PBUH) established many important practices and teachings that are still followed by Muslims today. These include the five pillars of Islam, which are the declaration of faith (shahada), prayer (salat), fasting during Ramadan (sawm), giving to charity (zakat), and the pilgrimage to Mecca (hajj).
