Fidak by Bibi Fatima Zehra (s.a)

The sermon delivered by Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) at Ghadeer Khumm is considered to be one of the most
significant events in Islamic history. It took place on the 18th of Dhu al-Hijjah in the year(632 CE)

The sermon of Fadak is a speech delivered by Fatimah bint Muhammad, the daughter of the Prophet Muhammad, in which she defended her claim to the ownership of the land of Fadak, which had been gifted to her by her father before his death. The sermon was delivered in the court of the caliph Abu Bakr, who had refused to acknowledge her ownership of the land. Background: Fadak was a fertile oasis located near Medina, and it had been gifted to Fatimah by her father, the Prophet Muhammad, before his death. However, after the death of the Prophet, Abu Bakr, the first caliph of Islam, refused to acknowledge her ownership of the land and claimed that it belonged to the Muslim community as a whole. Sermon: Fatimah, accompanied by her husband Ali and her two sons, Hasan and Husayn, went to the court of Abu Bakr to assert her claim to the land of Fadak. She began her speech by praising Allah and sending blessings upon her father, the Prophet Muhammad. She then reminded Abu Bakr of her father's gift of Fadak to her and quoted several hadiths (sayings) of the Prophet Muhammad in support of her claim. Fatimah argued that the land of Fadak was not public property but rather a personal gift from her father to her. She also pointed out that her father had given gifts to other members of his family, including Abu Bakr himself, and had never asked for them back. Fatimah also emphasized the fact that the Prophet Muhammad had explicitly stated that his family should not be deprived of their rights and that they should be treated with kindness and respect. Despite Fatimah's eloquent arguments, Abu Bakr remained unmoved and refused to return the land of Fadak to her. Fatimah, Ali, and their family continued to press their claim, but they were eventually forced to give up their efforts. Significance: The sermon of Fadak is significant because it sheds light on the early disputes and power struggles within the Islamic community after the death of the Prophet Muhammad. It also highlights the importance of the issue of inheritance and the rights of the Prophet's family in Islamic law and tradition. The sermon is often cited as an example of Fatimah's courage and eloquence, and it has inspired many Muslim women throughout history to assert their rights and speak out against injustice.
