Hazrat Bibi Fatima (SA) was the youngest daughter of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and his wife Hazrat Khadija (SA). She was born in Mecca in 605 AD and is considered one of the most important female figures in Islamic history.

Bibi Fatima (SA) was known for her piety, intelligence, and devotion to her family. She was deeply loved and respected by her father, who referred to her as "the leader of all women in Paradise." She was also married to Hazrat Ali (RA), the fourth caliph of Islam and the cousin and son-in-law of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

Bibi Fatima (SA) played a significant role in the early years of Islam. She supported her father's mission and teachings, and was one of the first people to embrace Islam. She was also present during many of the major events in Islamic history, including the migration to Medina and the battles of Badr and Uhud.

After the death of her father, Bibi Fatima (SA) faced many challenges and difficulties. She suffered greatly from the loss of her father and her husband, and was also subjected to mistreatment and oppression by some of the rulers of the time.
